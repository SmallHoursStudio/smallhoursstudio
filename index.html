Prayerful

the door is always open — a quiet companion for daily prayer. One gentle prompt each day, a moment with God, and something to carry back into the world. Selah.

Spiritual iOS & Android Coming soon
It is not a Bible study tool or a content platform. It is a quiet companion — a single gentle prompt each day, space to pray without hurry, and a piece of scripture or reflection to ease you back into ordinary time.

The name comes from a posture, not a programme. The subtitle — Selah — is a Hebrew pause marker woven through the Psalms. A breath. A moment to stop and be present. That is all Prayerful asks of you.

How it works

Every session has three moments, each with its own shape:

  • Threshold — an invitation appears. You step in deliberately. The prompt fades in from a brief, quiet emptiness — the doorway.
  • Presence — the prompt sits on a bare screen. No chrome, no timer. You pray for as long as you need. A small sage dot waits at the bottom, available whenever you are ready.
  • Return — a piece of scripture, a quote, or an original line eases the transition back to your day. A closing word holds its own moment of space before the day resumes.

Design and tradition

Prayerful draws on the same instincts as Surface — negative space, gentleness, absence of pressure or gamification. The design language is identical: warm near-white in light mode, deep candlelit brown in dark mode. Two apps that feel like the same quiet room.

The content draws from Catholic, Protestant, Anglican, Reformed, and contemplative voices — explicitly Christian, not bound to any single tradition. Content includes prompts in four translations (NIV, ESV, NRSV, KJV), scripture, quotes from theologians across the centuries, and original lines.

What it doesn't do

No streaks. No badges. No red days. No leaderboards. No pressure. The Practice screen is a dot grid — a quiet mirror of what you've done, not a dashboard of what you haven't. The app is designed so that after months of daily use, it still feels fresh and nourishing.

Pricing

Free, permanently. There is an optional in-app purchase to support the studio — framed as supporting the ministry of the app, not as a transaction.

Surface

A calm daily habit for keeping your home's surfaces clear. One small act, offered gently, every day.

Habit iOS & Android Free
App Store Play Store

What it is

Surface is not a cleaning app. It is not a task manager. It is a quiet companion — offering one small, intentional act each day. Return something to where it belongs. Clear one surface. Nothing more.

The design draws from Swedish lagom (just the right amount) and Japanese kanso (simplicity). Every screen is an argument for the habit it is trying to build.

How it works

  • Each day, Surface assigns you one small task from a growing library
  • A gentle hold interaction marks it complete — unhurried, intentional
  • A reflection appears: a quote, a haiku, or an original line
  • Progress is shown as a quiet dot grid — no streaks, no red days, no pressure

What it doesn't do

No badges. No leaderboards. No streaks. No notifications you didn't ask for. No data collected. Surface is designed so that over time, you need it less. That is success, not failure.

Pricing

Free, permanently. There is an optional in-app purchase to support the studio — framed as appreciation, not transaction.

Privacy Policy

Surface · Last updated May 2026

Surface is designed to work entirely on your device. It does not collect, store, or transmit any personal data.

What the app stores

Your preferences, configured spaces, notification settings, and tidy history are saved locally on your device in a file that never leaves it. This data is not accessible to the app's developers.

Notifications

If you enable daily prompts, Surface schedules notifications locally on your device. No data is sent to any server to do this.

Purchases

If you choose to support Surface through the optional in-app purchase, the transaction is handled entirely by Apple or Google. Small Hours Studio does not process or store any payment information.

Third-party services

Surface does not use analytics, advertising, crash reporting, or any third-party SDKs that collect data.

Children

Surface does not knowingly collect data from anyone, including children.

Contact

If you have any questions about this policy, please get in touch at privacy@smallhours.studio.

This policy may be updated if the app's data practices change. Any changes will be reflected here with an updated date.

Made in the
small hours between.

There's a particular quality to the hours after the house goes quiet. That's where most of this was built — after work, after family, after church. In the margins. Hence the name.

I'm a developer and designer. I work a full-time job, I'm married to Caroline, we have a three-year-old, and faith is a significant part of how I move through the world. The studio grew out of all of that — not despite it.

The things I make tend to be small and considered. I'm drawn to apps that do one thing well, that feel unhurried, that treat the person using them with some respect. I don't start a project because there's a gap in the market. I start one because something feels genuinely worth making — a small act of creativity I hope might land in someone's life and mean something.

I build for Android, iOS, and Steam, using Godot. I design everything myself. Caroline is involved in concept and testing — she has better instincts than me about whether something actually feels good to use.

If you want to get in touch — about an app, a bug, or anything else — hello@smallhours.studio is the right place.
